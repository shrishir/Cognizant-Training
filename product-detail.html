The Walnut Side Table is a celebration of material honesty. We start with slabs of sustainably-harvested black walnut from small family-run mills in the American Midwest, where the wood is air-dried for a minimum of two years before entering our workshop.

Each table is shaped by hand using traditional joinery — no dowels, no hidden hardware. The tapered legs are mortise-and-tenon connected to the top, creating a bond that strengthens with age. The final surface is sanded through eight grits and hand-rubbed with pure linseed oil, letting the walnut's natural lustre speak for itself.

Because we use natural wood, each table's grain pattern is completely unique. Slight variations in color and figure are a mark of authenticity, not a defect.

Cleaning: Wipe with a slightly damp cloth and dry immediately. Avoid harsh chemicals, solvents, or abrasive cleaners.

Conditioning: Re-oil every 6–12 months with pure linseed or walnut oil to maintain the finish and protect the wood.

Environment: Keep away from direct sunlight and heating vents. Wood naturally expands and contracts with humidity — this is normal.

Material: 100% solid black walnut. No plywood, MDF, or veneers. Hardware-free joinery.
